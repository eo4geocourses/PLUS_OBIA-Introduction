Boundaries in images

Imgage data neither 'contain' nor store boundaries

  • But boundaries can be extracted from image data by..
  • image segmentation techniques
  • Boundaries occur in several scales and can be used for better image understanding

A spatial principle in image analysis

Spatial Autocorrelation

  • Tobler's first law of Geography ("near things are more related than distant ones")
  • Neighbouring pixels tend to be similar
  • Boundaries can be detected at discontinuities or gradients
  • ...
  • Objects occur on distinct 'levels'; here: levels of parts (eyes, beard, forehead), level of whole (head)

A spatial principle in image analysis...

  • Without spatial autocorrelation the number of grouping options would be arbitrary and +/- infinite
  • With spatial autocorrelation it becomes a matter of optimisation and ambivalence ('ill-posed')
